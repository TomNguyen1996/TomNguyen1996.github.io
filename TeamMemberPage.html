Jacek Adams

Jacek Adams is expecting to graduate December 2020 with a B.S. in Computer Science and certifications in Artificial Intelligence and Database Systems. He speaks English, Spanish, Chinese, and Japanese. He's interested in manufacturing software, data analytics for legal businesses, and blacksmithing. He took his senior year of high school from a public school in Beijing (人大附中 for inquiring minds) and would like to pursue a career that takes advantage of his language skills. His native language is Spanish; he grew up speaking it at home and learned English at preschool and kindergarten. His language skills were what drew him to this project in particular, as the tetraski team is looking to make an Olympic sport.

Courtney Crums

'My name is Courtney Crum. I'm a Computer Science Major at the University of Utah, expected to graduate December 2020. I was born and raised in the Salt Lake Valley and I only know how to tell cardinal directions when there are mountains nearby. I am the second of 6 children, ranging from 13 to 27. I grew up skiing with my family and am thrilled to have had the opportunity to work on this capstone project with the Adaptive Sports Team to expand access to the TetraSki chair. My interests include role-playing games, both digital and tabletop, piano, and working on software that can really make an impact. I am employed at Audio Enhancement in West Jordan, Utah currently as a Software Developer, though I have worked many positions there over my 11 years of employment there. Here at the University of Utah I have been a part of many different majors before settling into Computer Science and since joining I have never looked back.'
