TETRASKI

The Tetradapt TetraSki is, at its simplest level, a chair with skis attached. These skis are controllable through the use of powerful actuators and the chair has several hydraulic mechanisms to assist smooth loading onto a lift chair. Its controls include a joystick for those with enough motor function, and a “sip and puﬀ” control scheme, where users blow or suck on a straw to turn the skis, allowing any patient who is able to breathe to be able to ski. The TetraSki is designed to make the patient’s experience as smooth as possible, and the brunt of the technical work falls upon the TetraSki “Instructor”, or control partner. This instructor skis behind the ski chair with two methods to control the chair in the event of a dangerous situation: a remote control that can steer the chair, and a rope tethered directly to the back of the chair. Many TetraSki instructors-in-training have experience with other adaptive sports equipment on the mountain which hinders far more than it helps when using the TetraSki; no other piece of equipment has its own control scheme, and so the remote control is left almost wholly unused. Even worse, the physics of the Tetraski are such that if an instructor pulls on the tether at a time where another piece of equipment requires that input, the chair may tip. It is the goal of this capstone project to provide support to the official TetraSki team by creating a training simulation for TetraSki instructors.
